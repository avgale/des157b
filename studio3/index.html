pH Levels

Ocean acidification is one of climate change's many tragedies, caused by the ocean's uptake of CO2. A decrease in pH, which corresponds to more acidity, creates a hostile environment for species that are acclimated to a more alkaline environment.

Sea Surface Temperature

Another side affect of the ocean's uptake of greenhouse gases is a rise in sea surface temperatures. This adds to the difficulties marine species experience when trying to adapt to a rapidly changing environment.
